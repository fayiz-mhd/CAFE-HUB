Famous Kerala Foods

1. Puttu and Kadala Curry:

A quintessential Kerala breakfast. Puttu is a steamed cylindrical rice cake layered with grated coconut, served with Kadala Curry (a spicy black chickpea curry made with coconut milk).

2. Appam with Stew (Ishtu)

Appam is a soft, bowl-shaped pancake made from fermented rice batter and coconut milk, featuring a thick, fluffy center and lace-like crispy edges. It is typically served with Ishhu

3. Malabar Parotta

A famous street food and layered flatbread, known for its flaky, crispy, and multi-layered texture. It is a perfect accompaniment for rich curries.

4. Idiyappam (Noolappam):

Also known as string hoppers, these are delicate, steamed rice noodles shaped like nests, often eaten with stew or sweet coconut milk (Paal Kanjiyum). Malabar Parotta: A famous street food and layered flatbread, known for its flaky, crispy, and multi-layered texture. It is a perfect accompaniment for rich curries.

5. Karimeen Pollichathu

A signature delicacy where Karimeen (Pearl Spot fish) is marinated in a spicy masala (spice paste), wrapped tightly in a banana leaf, and then grilled or pan-fried. The banana leaf infusion gives it a unique, smoky aroma.

6. Naadan Beef Fry (Beef Ularthiyathu):

A fiery and aromatic dish where small pieces of beef are slow-roasted or stir-fried with a generous blend of spices, curry leaves, ginger, garlic, and coconut slivers until they are dark brown and semi-dry.
